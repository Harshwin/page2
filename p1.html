Developement of a parallel adaptive solver



An incompressible three-dimensional finite volume second-order accurate projection method Navier-Stokes solver was developed and parallelization of the solver with domain decomposition was done. Development of a geometric VOF method to capture the interface and the block-based adaptive mesh refinement (AMR) of the solver for massively parallel physics-based adaptive solving is currently underway.

Code is written in OOPs of C++.
Parallelization is done using MPI.
Parallel simulations are being tested on the supercomputer (SahasraT - Cray XC40).

This project is being carried out under the guidance of Prof. Gaurav Tomar.
